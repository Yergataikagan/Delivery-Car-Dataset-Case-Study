# Delivery-Car-Dataset-Case-Study

This Excel project was built to simulate a real-world business analyst task: inheriting an incomplete, uncleaned dataset from a previous analyst and delivering actionable insights on vehicle efficiency across key cities for a logistics company.

What was done:

1. Identified and resolved data quality issues in the raw dataset, including: Empty rows and blank fields; Missing service and retirement dates; Incorrect data (e.g. malformed VIN numbers); Formatting inconsistencies


2. Solutions Applied: Built a clean, analysis-ready dataset; Flaged markers to identify retired vs. active vehicles; Assumptions to handle missing date values; Checked for errors to validate data integrity across all records;


3. Insights & Visualization: Built pivot tables and charts to surface key business insights across 4 cities (Los Angeles, New York, San Francisco, Dallas) and 3 vehicle makes (Ford, Chevrolet, RAM).


2.**Key Insights**

Ford poor fuel efficiency :Ford Transit Cargo Vans average only 15 MPG, significantly lower than Chevrolet (25 MPG) and RAM (24 MPG)


LA and SF have the highest fuel costs, at $4.70/gallon, roughly 40% more expensive than Dallas ($3.31/gallon)

High number of retired Ford vehicles: 4 out of 6 retired vehicles are Ford Transit Cargo Vans, suggesting higher turnover in the Los Angeles fleet

Aging LA vehicles. Los Angeles has the oldest average fleet placement date, indicating vehicles there have been in service the longest


3. **Dataset**

Raw fictional dataset of 20 delivery vehicles across 4 US cities, structured across 5 sheets: https://github.com/Yergataikagan/Delivery-Car-Dataset-Case-Study/blob/main/Carsfuel.xlsx
